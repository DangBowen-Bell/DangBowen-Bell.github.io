Text to 3D Human Motion Generation

Abstract

Text-to-motion generation is an interesting topic in the filed of computer vision, which aims to generate realistic 3D human motion from textual descriptions and has a wide range of applications in filmmaking, video games, and digital humans. Unlike static image or object generation, this task involves capturing temporal dynamics, physical plausibility, and fine-grained semantic alignment between language and motion. While recent methods have shown promising results, challenges still remain in preserving the correct ordering, duration, and completeness of the described actions. These limitations motivate the need for models that can more explicitly reason about the temporal structure inherent in natural language.
